> Why does this matter?_

  • ▷ Saying just "Hello" and waiting forces the other person to respond before you even ask your question. It's like calling someone and putting them on hold.
  • ▷ People are busy and async. They might see your "Hi" hours later. If your full question was there, they could have answered immediately.
  • ▷ It breaks focus. A "Hi" notification pulls someone out of deep work just to reply "Hey, what's up?" — that's two context switches for nothing.
  • ▷ It slows everyone down. The back-and-forth "Hi" → "Hey" → "Can I ask something?" → "Sure" dance can waste 15-30 minutes of elapsed time.
  • ▷ Your message IS your greeting. Starting with "Hi [Name], I have a question about X..." is perfectly polite AND efficient.
💡 Pro Tip:
A great Teams message looks like this:

Hi [Name]! I'm working on [project/topic] and ran into [specific issue]. Could you help with [specific ask]? Here's what I've tried: [details]. Thanks!
